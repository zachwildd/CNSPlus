## Page-level render constraints

Page 3 inherits the issue's working **costume**, **tail**, **slave-collar /
handcuff**, and **inked-comic style** conventions from
[page 1's *Page-level render constraints*](../page-1/panels.md). Page-3-specific
overrides and additions follow.

- **Setting flip — we are in the linking chamber.** The setting carried over
  from [page 2 ▭₅–₇](../page-2/panels.md). Cold blue-white industrial overhead
  light; steel-plate floor; a **circle of restraint chairs / cradles** around a
  **central tail-port apparatus**; banks of cabling running into the floor.
  No more warm-pool / floor-indicator language from pages 1–2 — we are at the
  bottom; the descent is over.
- **Hoods come DOWN on this page.** Pages 1–2 kept every prisoner hooded. On
  page 3 the system strips them. **This is the first time the reader sees
  Bursun's and Yaharia's faces**, and the first time the other cybervators
  are individuated rather than a hooded chorus. Treat the face reveals as
  *quiet* events — the visual is the beat, no balloon needs to call it out.
- **Tail plugs are a new visual element.** Each chair / cradle in the
  circle has a **tail port** at its rear; the prisoner's tail is **plugged
  into** the port. Cabling runs from the port down through the floor toward
  the central apparatus. This is the *linking* mechanism — render it as
  industrial-medical, not magical. Open: confirm whether the plug is a
  clamp, a needle-array, a socket, etc. — for now the prompts say "the tail
  inserted into a port at the back of the chair".
- **All panels equally sized.** [`script.md`](script.md) explicitly directs
  *"All panels are equally sized"* — a **uniform grid** for the page, not the
  varied-size composition of pages 1–2. Suggested layout: **2 columns × 3
  rows** (or 3 × 2). The panel-type glyphs `◰ ◳ ◲` describe shot content
  within each equal-sized cell, not the cell's physical dimensions.
- **Two scientist roles.** Per [page 4](../page-4/script.md) the observing
  scientists watch monitors **in a viewing room above** the chamber. Page 3
  needs both:
  1. **Chamber-floor technicians (▭₁, ▭₅)** — gloved, masked, in the circle
     with the prisoners; they pull hoods and administer needles.
  2. **Observing scientist (▭₆)** — at a console **in the viewing room
     above**; the one with the speaking line, watching Bursun's monitor.
  The script calls both "scientist". Vault them once their visual identity
  is locked. Working guess: lab coats over heavy aprons, surgical masks,
  rubber gloves, no tails (distinct caste). See *Open items* below.
- **Bursun's eyes are closed from ▭₁ onward.** The script's *"Bursun sits
  down but Bursun has his eyes closed"* is not just a sleep posture — it is
  the **visible sign** of his already-hyper-linked state. By ▭₁ his hood is
  being pulled by a scientist and his eyes are **already closed**. He does
  not resist; he is not present. The ▭₆ reveal recasts every prior panel of
  his eyes-closed as *"he wasn't asleep — he was already gone."*
- **Yaharia has no dialogue on this page.** The script gives her none, and
  her telepathic chatter on pages 1–2 contrasted against silence on page 3
  is the **tonal shift** that lets the scientist's single spoken line land
  as the page's narrative payoff. Her face does the work this page — first
  reveal, eyes-open-while-everyone-else-closes, the small composed smile.
  Open: confirm with ⦿ᴬ. If she should have a telepathic line, ▭₃ or ▭₄
  are the natural slots.
- **Balloon device — telepathic vs. speech, third register introduced.**
  - **Yaharia's balloons:** none on this page. Her wavy-telepathic
    convention from [page 2](../page-2/panels.md) is held in reserve so it
    does not steal from ▭₆.
  - **The observing scientist's balloon (▭₆):** **standard speech** — smooth
    oval outline, pointed tail to an open mouth. First scientist line in
    the comic; sets the convention for *system-voice* speech going forward.
  - **Bursun:** silent, as always.
- **Cyberspace teaser (▭₆ monitor).** The script's reveal beat is
  *"This ones already hyper linked"*. The strongest way to land this is
  visual: **the scientist's monitor shows Bursun's cyberspace avatar**
  (per [page 4 description](../page-4/script.md): glowing flowing hair,
  no mouth, eyes only, prison number on the chest) **while the other
  monitors still show the prisoners' physical bodies**. The reader gets a
  preview of cyberspace through the screen before [page 4](../page-4/script.md)
  opens inside it.
